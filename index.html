Use this as a reference to understand what works with your natural colouring. Colour analysis is about empowerment, not restriction.

The Light Summer palette contains thousands of colours. The swatches here are carefully chosen examples of what to look for: neutral undertone, medium intensity, light value. Your colours sit closest to neutral. Use these as a visual compass, not a checklist.

Screen tip: For the most accurate colour reference, turn off your blue light filter and set your screen brightness to around 80 percent or higher. On iPhone look for Night Shift under Settings > Display & Brightness. On Samsung look for Eye Comfort Shield under Settings > Display.

Online shopping tip: When unsure about a colour, try asking an AI assistant: "Is [Brand] [Product] in [Colour Name] cool, warm, or neutral toned? Does it suit Light Summer?" Keep in mind AI pulls from across the internet and isn't always accurate, since sources can conflict. Always check samples in store when possible.

Makeup & Hair
Your Light Summer guide to beauty: neutral undertone, medium intensity, light value. Products listed are current, findable options across a range of price points worth looking for.

Product recommendations are included as a helpful starting point. Always verify before purchasing, as formulas and shade names can change. Check the product description directly, or ask a consultant in store.

Lips
Your most flattering lip colours are neutral, medium-intensity pinks and roses, light to medium in depth. A colour that inches slightly warm or slightly cool still works beautifully, as long as it doesn't commit fully to either side.
Everyday: soft, neutral pinks
RMS Beauty Lip2Cheek Demure
Charlotte Tilbury Pillow Talk
e.l.f. Glow Reviver Lip Oil, Pink Quartz
Look for products described as soft pink, nude-pink, or neutral pink. These are your most wearable everyday options, colour without ever looking done up. Tip: any of these worn lightly give a beautiful your-lips-but-better result.
Polished: nude-rose and mauve
RMS Beauty Lip2Cheek Spell
e.l.f. Glossy Lip Stain, Rose Rizzin'
ILIA Color Block Lipstick, Wild Rose
Look for nude dusty rose, or neutral mauve products. These are your go-to for work, evenings out, and any occasion where you want your lip to say a little more. Note: ILIA's formula runs more full-coverage and pigmented than the others here, so a lighter hand goes a long way.
Evening: neutral red
RMS Beauty Lip2Cheek Beloved
Kjaer Weis Lipstick, KW Red
Look for a true, neutral red with a sheer or buildable finish rather than a fully opaque matte. Beloved is described by RMS as a true red, sheer enough to build up gradually rather than committing to full intensity in one swipe. KW Red is Kjaer Weis's own signature true red, a creamy formula that builds from a sheer wash up to a fuller satin finish, for when you want a little more richness than a balm gives. Tip: pat rather than swipe to keep the intensity in your medium range.
Your "nude" lip: neutral, not warm or ashy
Your version of a natural lip is a soft neutral pink or nude-rose, never fully warm or fully cool. Look for products described as neutral pink or nude-rose. Try: RMS Beauty Lip2Cheek in Spell worn lightly, or Charlotte Tilbury Pillow Talk worn alone.
Blush
Soft neutral pinks bring a natural flush without tipping warm or cool. If you enjoy wearing bronzer, choose a soft, neutral-toned bronzer rather than anything golden or orange, and apply it low on the cheek rather than sweeping it across the whole face.

Foundation & Tinted Moisturiser
Foundation rule: neutral undertone first.
What to look for
This applies across every skin tone and depth. If you can't find a true neutral match, a slight lean toward warm or cool is fine as long as it's not extreme. Avoid anything described as strongly golden, or strongly pink and ashy. Tip: swatch on your jawline in natural light. A match that blends seamlessly is right; one that pulls noticeably orange or noticeably pink is off.
Eyes
Your eyes have real room to play in soft blues, teals, and mauves. A soft eye works beautifully with a stronger lip, and a stronger eye pairs well with a quieter lip.
Eyeshadow: what works
Base eyeshadow: soft ivory, rose beige, pale grey, soft lilac. Depth: stone grey, smoky mauve, dusty violet, slate sky, sea pine. Accents: soft periwinkle, dusty turquoise, medium teal, garnet rose. Avoid: heavily golden or bronze metallics, and anything fully matte-black.
Eyeliner
Soft charcoal-grey, slate, or navy are your most versatile liners, each giving a different depth. Tip: a softly smudged grey liner with a neutral pink lip creates an effortless, natural finish.
Mascara
Soft black or dark brown mascara are your best choices. A very warm brown can introduce unwanted warmth; keep it neutral-to-cool rather than golden-brown.
Brows & Beard
Brow & beard guidance
Match your brow product to your natural hair colour or go one shade darker. Look for neutral taupe or neutral brown depending on your depth. Avoid anything strongly golden, red, or auburn-toned. Tip: ask for "neutral" or "ashy-neutral" when shopping brow pencils or powders.
Hair Colour
Your most beautiful hair colour is often the one you were born with.
Your best hair colours
Your naturals: blonde through light-medium brown, kept neutral rather than strongly warm or ashy. Darker hair tends to overwhelm this light placement, so if you naturally have dark hair, work closely with a colourist rather than going lighter alone. Avoid: very dark or black hair, which reads much heavier than anything else in your natural colouring.
Embracing silver & grey
Soft, light silver can be beautiful on Light Summer when it comes in naturally, since it echoes your palette's own light, neutral quality. Tip: a violet-based toning shampoo keeps it soft and neutral rather than yellow.
What to ask your colourist
Ask for "neutral toned," "not too warm, not too ashy," or show them your palette directly. Tip: when in doubt, go lighter rather than darker.
